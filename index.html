About Ella

Ella Ferrin is an incoming senior SCOM (public relations) and SMAD (interactive design) double major. She was elected as the president of both LPH (Lambda Pi Eta) and PRSSA (Public Relations Student Society of America) for next year. She is an aspiring PR practitioner and is learning more about interactive, UX design more everyday. Her dream job is working for the communications team for a public consumer company.

Originally from Roseville, Minnesota, Ella moved to Northern Virginia (NOVA) in middle school. Since moving to NOVA, Ella has excelled academically, been involved in extracurriculars and participated in athletic activities. Ella enjoys doing well in her studies to earn honors, joining and leading organizations that she is passionate about, and playing basketball, but just for fun now!

She has been a Resident Advisor (RA) for the past two years and has loved building community and making connections with JMU first-years. Next year, she has been promoted to a Hall Director, meaning she is in charge of maintaining the safety of and building community within an entire building, along with supervising a staff of RAs.

Ella is the oldest of four siblings, but somehow the shortest at 5'10". Outside of school, she enjoys hammocking on the Quad, playing with her cats, reading a good book, hanging out with her siblings and boyfriend, and making crafts.

Internship Experience

Senior Capstone Project

During the end of her senior year of high school, Ella choose to do her senior Capstone Project with Orbital ATK, now Northrop Grumman. In this position, Ella worked out of their Dulles, VA office for the communications team. She drafted social media posts, wrote an article that ended up on the website front page, and was even able to attend and help with communications for a rocket launch. This experience solidifed her desire to work in the communications field and made her even more passionate about and excited for her studies.

Northrop Grumman

After her Capstone Project, Ella was invited to be a full-time, paid intern in the summer of 2019. She did a lot of the same tasks, including writing social media posts and articles, but she also learned about internal and crisis communications, event planning and website and search engine optimization (SEO). She was also able to interact with other interns that summer and enjoyed getting to know other driven, young individuals.

Bluestone Communications

Bluestone Communications is JMU's nationally-affiliated, student-run public relations firm. Ella started as an intern in the fall of her junior year and was promoted to an Account Coordinator in the spring. She will be stepping into the role of Assistant Diretor of Public Relations next fall. Her work at the firm has included creating editorial calendars and graphics, maintaining client relations and writing monthly reports, and updating client websites with timely information.

Videography

Day-in-My-Life Montage

For my videography class this semester, we were tasked with creating a video montage that reflected a significant aspect of what JMU means to us. The goal of the video was to clearly convey the essence of the aspect I chose and evoke the intended feeling. I chose to create a video that showed my feelings of lonliness and longing to spend time with my boyfriend back at home.

Public Service Announcement - Look Up

Another assignment from my videography class was to create a public service announcement (PSA). The PSA could focus on a issue or need within JMU, the local community or the world. I chose to focus mine on how much we miss out on each day because we have a screen in front of our face or we are on our phone, hoping to encourage people to not miss out on the little things.

Interview with B-roll - LSAS

Our final project in my videography class was to conduct an interview and get b-roll footage highlighting a community person, organization, place or activity. I chose to film a video about Loudoun School for Advanced Studies (LSAS), the private school that my mom teaches at. I was able to interview her, along with another teacher and get some additional footage while there.

Spring Break Montage

Each spring break, I have traveled to Cape Canaveral, Florida to visit my grandparents and have brought a friend or two along each time. Capturing the memories on these trips have been super fun for me and also a great way to be able to look back on our adventures and great week. Stay tuned for this year's montage!
